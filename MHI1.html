Beauty? Beauty? I have no idea of that. I'm not too aware of myself anymore. But I want to be beautiful. "Isn't it enough to be alive?", they ask, but I can't answer because I am not alive if I am not beautiful to people. No wonder I can't see myself anymore. Clearly the soldering is fading because I smell something and it is ugly. I smell ugliness in being loveless like I am and that ugliness smells like smoke. And it hurts the body, smelling that ugliness. I wasn't created to be beautiful but the change forced me to be beautiful. The change hurt. It smelled like vile trash and vomit. And more smoke. Changing is ugly, and forces you to pretend it's beautiful and that you don't really care. Friends, but never lovers after that change. You aren't attractive when you're a political statement. It felt as though if I didn't change I would keep hurting. But it hurts to be lonely and my perfect self as well. I see other people get easy love after the change, and I wonder what I did wrong. I am truly and unendingly loveless because of the change. And yet I have to pretend I am happy. Because the facade will weaken to the non-changed. I am unendingly loveless. Some days I wish I was treated more like a god. I wish I had a group of worship. I'd hope they'd bow down to me and make me worse. I'm tired of being perfect.
